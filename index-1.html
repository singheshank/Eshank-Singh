Causes

Emissions from Vehicles

Cars, trucks, buses, and other vehicles emit a variety of pollutants, including carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, particulate matter, and volatile organic compounds. These pollutants contribute significantly to urban air pollution, especially in densely populated areas.
Industrial Emission

Factories and industrial facilities release pollutants such as sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, particulate matter, and volatile organic compounds into the air. Industries like manufacturing, power generation, and chemical production are major contributors to air pollution.
Burning of Fossil Fuels

Burning coal, oil, and natural gas for energy production releases pollutants like sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and carbon dioxide into the atmosphere. Power plants, heating systems, and industrial processes that rely on fossil fuels are significant sources of air pollution.
Agricultural Activities

Agriculture contributes to air pollution through the release of ammonia from livestock waste, as well as the use of fertilizers and pesticides, which can release nitrogen oxides and volatile organic compounds into the air.
Construction Activities

Construction activities generate dust and emissions from equipment and machinery, contributing to local air pollution levels, especially in urban areas undergoing development.
Waste Management

Improper disposal of waste, including open burning of garbage and landfill emissions, can release harmful pollutants into the air, including methane, volatile organic compounds, and particulate matter.
